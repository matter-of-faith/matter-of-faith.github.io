I recently went on a 10-day mission trip to Indonesia. This post is not meant to be an extensive summary of the trip, but to focus on a specific concept that was revealed to me throughout the 10 days I was there.

For those who might be unaware, Indonesia is a predominantly Muslim country. Although you are allowed to identify as a Christian, and though you are able to be part of a church, there still exists substantial persecution towards Christians. This persecution redoubles if said Christians begin to share the gospel and proselytize to Muslims. As one can probably imagine, many missionaries (and their ministries) in this country are under constant persecution. Thus, one of my greatest privileges of going on this trip was getting to know Christians who live in this country who dedicate their lives to making Christ known. 

What was so jarring was that I didn't just know about their persecution *only* through their stories (although they were so valuable to hear). I got to witness their persecution first-hand. I won't provide specific details here, but there was an instance on the trip where I got to see them try to defend their ministry from government. And when they were explaining to us what was happening, I felt a strange emotion that I don't think I've ever felt before. 

It was a mixture of sympathy, pity, and anger simultaneously. Not for myself, but on behalf of the Indonesian ministry we were with.  

At least, that was the best way I could describe it at the time. It didn't just stop there, however. For some reason, this amalgam of emotions seemed to transform into a brief surge of determination within myself that I've never felt before. Determination for what? I couldn't even precisely name. I felt this urge to, in some sense, "get to work." I felt this urge to return home and go all out in some kind of act of "vengeance" or "retribution" for all the things that the Indonesian ministry was going through. I even felt a brief restarting of my passion for my academic work back home, which I thought I had lost before going on my mission trip.

It was such a strange  confusing emotion for me to process, so I mainly kept quiet to myself about it until I came to a state of being able to articulate it reasonably to someone else. Eventually, I talked about it with my team leader (to whom I am very thankful for letting me rant incoherently).
