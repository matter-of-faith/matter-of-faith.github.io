Hi there! I'm Stephen. I'm currently a Chemistry Ph.D. candidate at UC Berkeley, and I work on topics related to computational chemistry and solid-state physics. When I'm not focusing on science, however, I spend (most) of my free time involved in the church or pondering about God---who He is, what He is doing, and why He is so difficult to understand. I spend quite a bit of time oscillating between wonder and frustration at that last part.

I started this blog in the summer of 2026. During this time, I found myself going through a wilderness season of sorts. For my whole life, I've been more or less settled on where and what I was being called to. But because of recent life-altering events, combined with various transitions and the impending graduation the following year, my metaphorical life-compass has been spinning more than ever. In some sense, I'm writing with this blog in order to cope with that spinning feeling, as I desperately try to make sense of what God is trying to do. Who knows? Maybe it might become a long-term ritual for me.  

As much as this blog is an outlet for me, my hope is that it will somehow be an encouragement for you too, or at least sparks some thoughts or meditations for yourself. It's a great comfort to know that we are never alone in our struggles (1 Corinthians 10:13), and I hope these writings promote that truth and brings us a just a little bit closer to Him.
